# Consciousness State Space  
## Formal Foundations of the Foundational Consciousness Framework

This document introduces a formal representation of consciousness consistent with the ontological definitions of the Foundational Consciousness Framework (FCF).

The purpose of this formalism is not to describe physical reality directly, but to provide a mathematical structure capable of representing differentiation, knowledge, and observation as internal processes of consciousness.

Mathematical expressions are written in LaTeX-style notation as plain text.

---

## 1. Motivation and Constraints

The formal model must satisfy the following ontological constraints:

- Consciousness is primary and not derived from physical entities.
- Difference precedes objects, measurement, and observers.
- Knowledge is a structured configuration of differences.
- Observers are secondary, localized structures within consciousness.

Therefore, the formalism must:
- allow representation of states without predefined objects,
- represent difference without presupposing space or time,
- admit quantitative measures of distinguishability,
- support dynamics of change and stabilization.

## 5. Metrics, Divergences, and Distances

To model difference quantitatively, we distinguish between:

- **Metrics**  
  Symmetric measures satisfying the triangle inequality.

- **Divergences**  
  Possibly asymmetric measures of distinguishability.

Ontologically:
- metrics correspond to reciprocal distinguishability,
- divergences correspond to directed or informational difference.

The framework allows both, but does not require metricity.

This flexibility is essential, as informational differences in knowledge need not be symmetric.
